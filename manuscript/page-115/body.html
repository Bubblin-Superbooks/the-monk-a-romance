If Thou hast known false Love's vexation,  
Or hast been exil'd from thy Nation,  
Or guilt affrights thy contemplation,  
       And makes thee pine,  
Oh! how must Thou lament thy station,  
       And envy mine!

'Were it possible' said the Friar, 'for Man to be so totally wrapped up in himself as to live in absolute seclusion from human nature, and could yet feel the contented tranquillity which these lines express, I allow that the situation would be more desirable, than to live in a world so pregnant with every vice and every folly. But this never can be the case. This inscription was merely placed here for the ornament of the Grotto, and the sentiments and the Hermit are equally imaginary. Man was born for society.
