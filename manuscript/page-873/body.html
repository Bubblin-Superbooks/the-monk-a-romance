Chapter V

——He was a fell despightful Fiend:  
Hell holds none worse in baleful bower below:  
By pride, and wit, and rage, and rancor keened;  
Of Man alike, if good or bad the Foe.  
             Thomson.

On the day following Antonia's death, all Madrid was a scene of consternation and amazement. An Archer who had witnessed the adventure in the Sepulchre had indiscreetly related the circumstances of the murder: He had also named the Perpetrator. The confusion was without example which this intelligence raised among the Devotees. Most of them disbelieved it, and went themselves to the Abbey to ascertain the fact.
